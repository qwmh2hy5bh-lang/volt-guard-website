Quick answer: power's out at night — what now?

  1. 1 Check the street: if neighbours are dark too, it's a power cut — call 105 (free national number).
  2. 2 Open the fuse board: look for a switch in the OFF or middle position — that circuit tripped.
  3. 3 Reset once only: unplug recent appliances on that circuit, switch fully OFF then ON.
  4. 4 Smell burning, repeated tripping, or sparking? Do NOT reset. Call us on 07508 711874.

How much does an emergency electrician cost in London?

Our standard hourly rate is £85 during normal working hours (Mon-Fri 8am-5pm). Evening calls (5pm-9pm) are £115/hour, weekends £125/hour, and night call-outs (9pm-8am) £140/hour. Minimum charge is 1 hour, then billed in 30-min increments. There's no call-out fee — you only pay if we work on your issue. How quickly can you respond to an emergency?

Our phone line is staffed 24/7 — including weekends and bank holidays. When you call, we'll give you a realistic ETA based on your location, the time of day and our current workload. We never promise a fake number we can't deliver. Arrival times are fastest in NW London where we're based (HA, UB, NW, W postcodes); further afield in central, north, east or south London — or in surrounding counties — we'll be honest about how long it will take.

How much does an EICR report cost?

Our fixed EICR prices include the official certified EICR report: £130 for a 1-bed flat, £165 for a 2-bed property, £195 for a 3-bed house, £245 for a 4-bed house. 5-bed properties and HMOs are quoted individually. How long does an EV charger installation take?

A standard home EV charger install takes 3–5 hours. We carry out a free pre-installation survey by phone or video first, install on the agreed day, and commission the unit ready for use before we leave. All units come with a 3-year hardware warranty.

Why does my RCD keep tripping?

The most common causes are: a faulty appliance leaking current to earth (washing machine, tumble dryer, fridge), water ingress in an outdoor socket, damaged cable insulation, or an overloaded circuit. Our diagnostic process isolates the cause in under an hour in 90% of cases.
